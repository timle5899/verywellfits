  • How Do Massage Guns Help?

    Massage guns work by providing percussive therapy, also known as vibration therapy, a type of massage that applies rapid bursts of pressure to muscle tissue. Percussive therapy can aid in post-workout recovery by increasing blood flow to specific areas, thereby reducing inflammation, relieving muscle tension and stiffness, breaking up knots that may have formed, and improving range of motion.

  • What to Look For

    • Noise: The motors that power massage guns can be surprisingly loud, which can take away from the spa-like relief they provide. To determine whether the massage gun you're considering is quiet or loud, check its decibel (dB) rating. Most of our top picks fall within the range of 30 to 60 decibels.
    • Intensity: Some users prefer more intensive percussive therapy to create a deep tissue massage effect. However, for acute injuries or strains, a massage gun with a higher intensity may be too powerful to allow the muscle to relax, which can lead to increased spasms, says certified physical therapist Kathleen Gassner, PT, DPT.
    • Weight: If a massage gun is too heavy, you may experience arm fatigue, which can hinder your ability to target specific areas of tension effectively. Ensure the model you're considering is lightweight enough for you to hold and maneuver easily with one hand.
    • Customization: Look for a massage gun with customizable, versatile features that allow you to target different muscle groups. We recommend purchasing a massage gun with several head attachments and adjustable angles, speeds, and levels for the best experience.

  • How We Tested

    To find the best massage guns, we tested 50 devices, under the guidance of two physical therapists, and at home as part of our regular workout recovery routines. During the testing process, we evaluate each massage gun based on the following key attributes.

    • Effectiveness: To test the effectiveness of massage guns, we use each one before and after our regular upper-body and lower-body workouts. Do our muscles feel warmer before workouts? Do we notice an improved range of motion? After exercising, we also note whether the massage gun helps decrease or prevent muscle fatigue.
    • Comfort: While using each massage gun, we evaluate how comfortable it is to hold and how heavy it feels. We also assess the strength of the pressure it applies and whether it feels comfortable on our muscles. If the handle, angle, or settings are customizable for a better experience, we also note that detail.
    • Ease of Use: We prioritize massage guns that make our sessions feel seamless and enjoyable. How easy is it to charge and set up the first time we use it? Can we quickly swap attachments and adjust settings? We also consider how easily we can reach different muscle groups.
    • Stall Force: Also known as "torque," stall force refers to the maximum pressure that can be applied to the head of the massage gun before the motor stalls. Massage guns with a higher stall force can provide a deep, satisfying massage. As we use each model, we note whether we can press down hard and long enough for a deep-tissue effect without stalling the motor. We also pay attention to how the stall force holds up over time—does it remain consistent, or does it weaken as the battery drains?
    • Portability: Some massage guns are more easily portable than others. During testing, we look for travel-friendly features, like a compact design, a carrying case with space for attachments, and a secure power button. We also consider whether the gun is quiet enough for use in public settings and whether the battery life is sufficient to last for an entire trip.
    • Value: To accurately rate each massage gun's value, we don't check the price until the end of each testing period, after we've evaluated it in all other key categories. Then, we weigh the cost against its features and performance. Would we pay this much for the massage gun, or is it overpriced?

Which Massage Gun Head Should I Use? 

It depends on the type of massage you're after. Here's our quick guide to each shape.

  • Large round attachments are best suited for larger muscle groups, such as the quadriceps, hamstrings, and glutes.
  • Two-prong attachments that resemble vertebrae can help relieve trigger points on either side of your spine, particularly in the lower back area.
  • Flathead attachments are ideal for targeting smaller areas, such as between the shoulder blades or the length of the IT band.
  • Bullet-shaped attachments can tackle tiny muscles in your hands and feet. Just be mindful of how much pressure you apply.
  • Small round heads are a great, all-around pick that works well on most of the body.

Note: Never press any massage gun attachment directly against your spine, bones, tendons, or ligaments.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can massage guns be harmful?

Yes, massage guns can be harmful if used improperly. Our experts recommend massaging a specific area for no more than two minutes at a time. A vibrating foam roller is another option for muscle relief that may be ideal for beginners or those who want less pressure than a massage gun.

Where should I not use a massage gun?

According to Marko, you should only use a massage gun on soft muscle tissue. Avoid bones, tendons, ligaments, and the trunk of your neck (near your carotid arteries), she adds. Also, avoid existing wounds and injuries, as the additional blood flow can worsen inflammation. If you’re experiencing joint pain, injury, acute muscle pain, or other musculoskeletal disorders, get clearance from your doctor before using a massage gun, says Marko.

How much should I spend on a massage gun?

You can expect to pay between $90 and $600 for a high-quality massage gun. Budget-friendly options are ideal for entry-level users testing a massage gun for the first time, but they may not be as durable as pricier options.

Is it okay to use a massage gun every day?

Yes, it's okay to use a massage gun every day so long as it's used properly and on healthy muscle tissue. In fact, daily use can be beneficial for muscle maintenance, especially for athletes and people with physically demanding routines.
