Looking to elevate your basketball game right from your own backyard? Whether you're honing your jump shot, engaging in family fun, or simply unwinding, the best portable basketball hoops offer the convenience of home-based play. Unlike wall-mounted or in-ground hoops, all you need for a portable basketball hoop is some space to play. Portable hoops are typically less expensive, easier to move around, and adjustable, making the game more accessible—especially for younger players.

To find the best options on the market, we first consulted basketball coaches and other experts in the field for their insight into what buyers should look for in a quality portable basketball hoop. Then, we researched over 30 hoops from 10 trusted brands to select the best ones. We also tested our best overall hoop in our driveway, playing with the whole family several times a week for over a month. We evaluated it for setup, ease of use, quality, and overall value.

What to Look for When Shopping for a Portable Basketball Hoop

  • Backboard Material: Backboards can come in three different materials: glass, acrylic, and polycarbonate. Glass is the professional standard for indoor basketball games; it’s durable, provides great ball bounce, and won't scratch or fade over time. However, it can easily break if hit with rocks, large branches, or other hard materials. Hoops with acrylic backboards are less expensive than glass ones and are usually more resilient. They’re ideal if you're worried about backboard damage. However, acrylic scratches easily, so it won't retain its new look or continue to provide great ball bounce over time. Polycarbonate backboards are also budget-friendly and can be even more durable than acrylic ones. Although the look and feel are similar to acrylic, the material is actually denser and tougher.
  • Durability: For a hoop you can use for years to come, look for one that is made of durable, shatterproof, and weatherproof materials. Adjustability is also a key factor, especially if you’re buying a hoop for young players and want it to last as they grow.
  • Placement: The type and size of the basketball hoop that’s right for you will largely depend on where you want to place it. Before selecting one, ensure you have an area that’s spacious, flat, and paved (if outdoors). Poolside hoops and over-the-door hoops are great options if you’re low on space or want to play indoors.
  • Price: The cost of portable hoops varies based on size, materials, and durability. According to German, portable basketball hoops that play just as well as permanent ones will likely cost you a pretty penny. However, they're worth the investment if you want a high-quality, sturdy hoop that lasts many years. If you’re unsure how much use the hoop will get, you may want to consider a budget-friendlier option with a smaller acrylic backboard.

Frequently Asked Questions

What size portable basketball hoop should I get?

Many portable basketball hoops are adjustable from 7.5 to 10 feet, which is a great range that can accommodate kids as they grow and improve their skills. Backboards can range from 44 to 72 inches, which is the regulation size at the professional level. A 44- to 54-inch backboard is a good size for most skill levels and should provide enough space for shooting, rebounding, and practicing other skills.

How much does a portable basketball hoop weigh?

Portable basketball hoops can vary in height and weight, but most range between 55 to 350 pounds, with the average being around 140 pounds. Systems with glass backboards are typically much heavier than those with acrylic or polycarbonate ones.

How do you store a portable basketball hoop?

If you’re not planning to use your portable basketball hoop during cold weather, storing it in a covered area like a garage, shed, or carport is best. If storing your hoop in a covered area isn’t possible, you should lower the rim to the lowest height, cover it with a tarp, and secure it with bungee cords.

How much should a portable basketball hoop cost?

You can expect to pay between $50 to $2,300 for a quality portable basketball hoop. Price will vary depending on the basketball hoop's type, material, and size. Indoor and over-the-door hoops tend to be budget-friendlier, while more durable, outdoor-proof hoops will typically cost you more.
