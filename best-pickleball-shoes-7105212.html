Pickleball—a blend of tennis, badminton, and table tennis—has taken the world by storm, but no one has embraced the trend quite like Floridians, especially those seeking community and an active lifestyle in retirement. So, when we set out to find the best pickleball shoes, we knew there were no better experts to test them than a team of 17 pickleball players—who just so happen to be my neighbors in Florida. While some claim to be beginners, they can absolutely run circles around you on the court (I know this firsthand). These intermediate to advanced players hit the court with their pickleball paddles multiple times a week, for two to three hours at a time under the hot Florida sun.

To identify the top pickleball shoes on the market, we had our 17 Floridian players each test a different pair for over a month, evaluating fit, flexibility, durability, comfort, support, and value. This allowed us to determine which shoes work best for every foot type and playing style—and which ones you should avoid altogether. We also consulted three pickleball experts to pinpoint the key features to look for in a quality pair of shoes.

What to Look for When Shopping for Pickleball Shoes

  • Traction: Traction refers to how well a shoe’s outsole creates friction between the shoe and the floor, particularly in wet or damp conditions. Good traction helps prevent slipping and sliding, and it also keeps you secure during sudden movements and direction changes. Some shoes also have pivot points in the sole, making sharp movements easier. Look for a pickleball shoe with plenty of traction so you can play confidently and injury-free. 
  • Comfort: Your pickleball shoes should feel comfortable, even after hours of play. Make sure your shoes are not so tight that the laces dig into the tops of your feet, or so loose that your feet slide around inside. A roomy toe box is important, as shoes that are too narrow can pinch your feet. Also, look for a breathable upper made from mesh that allows air circulation. Thick, foam cushioning in the midsole can help extend your playing time, too.
  • Support: Pickleball shoes are known for providing great lateral support, which is necessary for all those direction changes. But they also generally have lower arch support. As a result, some players will choose to add their own custom orthotics or insoles for flat feet. Your shoes should support your entire foot, including your arch. 
  • Durability: Pickleball shoes get a lot of wear and tear. Even if you don’t play regularly, the lateral motion and fast starts and stops mean your shoes are constantly working for you. Look for a pair with thick, sturdy rubber soles. Check the toes and heels to see if they have additional rubber that wraps around the shoe to protect against toe drags across the court. These features will help extend the life of your shoes, regardless of how often you pick up your pickleball paddle.
  • Price: You can expect to spend anywhere from $50 to $200 or more on quality pickleball shoes. Keep in mind that more budget-friendly options may not be as supportive or durable. Keep your budget and how often you play in mind when shopping for pickleball shoes.

How We Tested the Pickleball Shoes

To find the best pickleball shoes on the market, we first spoke with three pickleball experts to understand what to look for in a quality pair. Then, we had a team of pickleball players test 17 pairs of shoes for over one month outdoors in Florida's intense heat and humidity, bringing our total of tested pickleball shoes to 32 pairs. Our testers sprinted, jumped, and pivoted all while evaluating each pair for the following six key criteria.

  • Fit: To evaluate the fit of each pair of shoes, we note whether they are true to size and have an appropriate width and length after putting them on. Do we notice any pressure points or areas where the shoes feel too tight or too loose? While we're playing, we note if the shoe feels secure or if our feet slide around.
  • Comfort: Pickleball shoes need to be comfortable for extended play. To assess comfort, we note how they feel during games, particularly with different types of movement, such as quick stops and pivots. Do we experience any rubbing, chafing, or foot fatigue during or after games? Do the cushioning and breathability contribute to overall comfort while playing?
  • Support: When wearing the shoes, we consider whether they provide sufficient ankle and arch support. Do the shoes absorb shock and support stable lateral movement? Do we feel secure and protected from rolling or twisting our ankles?
  • Flexibility: We evaluate flexibility by noting whether the shoe allows us to move naturally during games. Do we feel restricted or stiff? Are the sole and upper flexible enough for quick movements?
  • Durability: Throughout the testing period, we check for signs of wear and tear on the upper and sole. If the shoes need cleaning, we note how easy it is to clean them. We also note if the shoes feel well-made with durable materials.
  • Value: We wait until the end of the testing period to check the price of each pair of shoes and note if they are priced fairly for their features. Would we purchase the shoes at full price, or wait for them to go on sale? Would we recommend the shoes to friends and family?

Frequently Asked Questions

Do pickleball shoes make a difference?

Pickleball shoes are designed to have good traction and support quick changes in direction during a game. Having shoes specific to pickleball can reduce your injury risk and allow you to pivot, jump, and shift side to side more easily.

Do I need special shoes for pickleball?

Yes, according to the pros. Pickleball-specific shoes provide lateral support, which protects against ankle rolls. While your shoes don’t need to be pricey, they should be designed for pickleball or at least other court sports.

What shoes should I wear when playing pickleball?

Recreational players or beginners still learning the rules of the game will likely want a heavier pickleball shoe—one that’s wider, with good support. As you move up in skill level, you may want a lighter shoe that allows for quicker movements.

Can I wear my tennis court shoes for pickleball?

You can, but it's not ideal. Tennis and pickleball shoes may look similar, but pickleball shoes are specifically designed for the sport, with better foot drag and stability, and added rubber in places where they tend to wear more heavily. Pickleball shoes are constructed to support the side-to-side movement common in a pickleball game. 

Can you wear running shoes for pickleball?

Our experts stress that wearing running shoes on the pickleball court is dangerous because of their lack of lateral support. A running shoe’s outsole has a different tread pattern, which may catch the pickleball court’s surface during play, causing you to trip or fall. Heilman says that the biggest injury in the game comes from tripping while backpedaling, which may happen more often if you're wearing running shoes.
