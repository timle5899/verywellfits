  • How Do Vibration Plates Work?

    A vibration plate vibrates while you sit, stand, or exercise on it. The vibrating motion recruits stabilizing muscles to contract and relax at a higher rate than usual, say personal trainer Holly Roser, CPT, and fitness coach Jen Rulon, MS. This forced resistance can improve muscle strength faster since you’re working harder and exerting more energy while on the plate. 

    Physical therapist Jorge Giral, PT, DPT, COMT, CSCS, says training on a vibration plate can also improve balance, flexibility, and overall mobility.

  • Vibration Plate Benefits

    A number of studies and experts suggest that the regular use of a vibration plate may deliver a host of health and fitness benefits.

    • Vibration plates can increase muscle contraction, leading to potential improvements in muscle strength, says Giral.
    • Vibration plates may help promote blood circulation, improving muscle warm-ups and cool-downs so they’re more effective and relaxing.
    • Vibration plate training may contribute to weight loss when combined with a healthy diet and exercise, though more research is still needed.
    • Whole-body vibration training may serve as an alternative exercise method for older adults that can improve stability and strengthen muscles. According to physical therapist Monica Saliu, PT, DPT, vibration plates are most beneficial for people with balance issues, often healthy adults over the age of 45.
    •  Physical therapist Theresa Marko, PT, DPT, MS, says vibration plates activate the stabilizing muscles, which are often weak in people with back pain.
    • Research suggests that total-body vibration training may improve muscle function and bone mineral density in older women. For this reason, Saliu says vibration plates are being used to slow the progression of conditions like osteoporosis.

  • How We Tested

    To find the best vibration plates on the market, we first spoke with experts—three physical therapists, one certified personal trainer, and one fitness coach—on the research behind the machines and what to look for when shopping for a high-quality option. We then spent hours testing 16 expert-approved vibration plates in The Verywell Testing Lab and at home, completing a series of exercises, including squats, lunges, planks, and more. When testing vibration plates, we keep the following key criteria in mind.

    • Ease of Use: We assess how easy it is to set up each vibration plate. Is any assembly required? If so, how long does it take? Then, we note the features and accessories—the display, capability to track stats, third-party app integration, Bluetooth connectivity, and more—and how easy they are to use. Vibration plates earn high scores if they are quick to set up and simple to use effectively. 
    • Adjustability: The best vibration plates offer simple adjustability. During testing, we note the number of settings and intensity levels each vibration plate offers. We also pay attention to how easily these settings can be adjusted mid-workout and whether any pre-programmed workouts are available. We prioritize vibration plates with a wide range of easy-to-adjust settings and intensity levels. 
    • Stability: During testing, we perform a series of three popular vibration plate exercises—lunges, squats, and planks—as well as a lower body hamstring and glute massage. As we complete the workout, we pay attention to how safe and stable we feel on the plate. We give high scores to vibration plates that are large enough for us to comfortably and confidently perform a range of exercises.  
    • Value: To assign each vibration plate a value score, we wait to look at the price until after our testing is complete. Vibration plates that receive a high score for value often cost the same or less than what we would consider paying for them based on their ease of use, adjustability, and stability.

How to Choose a Vibration Plate

  • Vibration Type: There are two main types of vibration plates: tri-plane plates and oscillation plates. Tri-plane plates vibrate up and down, side to side, and front to back. This type of plate is often seen in gyms and fitness centers. Oscillation plates move like a pendulum, rocking from one side to the other. This motion simulates walking and tends to be gentler, making it more suitable for therapy and general health than for sports or workouts. Sometimes, you can find plates with just linear or just vertical vibration.
  • Frequency and Amplitude: Frequency (measured in hertz, or Hz) refers to the number of vibration cycle movements within one second, while amplitude (measured in millimeters) is how far the plate moves up and down during each vibration. The higher the frequency and amplitude, the more intense your vibration plate session will be. You may want to opt for lower levels if your goal is recovery and higher levels to push yourself during workouts, says Rulon. 
  • Speeds: Choosing a vibration plate with adjustable speed levels allows you to customize each session to your specific needs. For instance, you may use a higher speed when standing still or doing lower-body exercises but lower speeds when sitting on the machine or doing upper-body exercises.
  • Weight Capacity: Most vibration plates are designed to support at least 230 pounds, but the weight capacity varies from machine to machine. Ideally, look for a machine that can support at least 300 pounds. Machines with a higher weight capacity also tend to be sturdier overall. 
  • Safety Features: Ensure your vibration plate is sturdy and won’t slip or slide around while in use. Suction cups, rubber feet, or included mats are common features that can help prevent unwanted movement. Look for a plate with good traction, featuring a non-slip rubber or carpeted surface. 
  • Features and Accessories: Some vibration plates have features like pre-programmed workouts, built-in speakers, and Bluetooth connectivity. Other plates come with added accessories, such as remote controls, exercise mats, resistance bands, and removable seats. It’s important to select a vibration plate that has the features and accessories you need to feel safe and achieve your goals. 
  • Price: You can expect to pay anywhere from $100 to over $3,000 for a high-quality vibration plate. Less pricey options may not be as sturdy or durable and may not have any additional features or accessories. Then again, not everyone needs all the bells and whistles found in pricier models.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best vibration plate for bone density? 

The best vibration plate for bone density operates between 12 and 90 hertz. Recent studies have found that vibration therapy is regarded as a safe, low-risk, non-pharmacological primary and adjunct treatment for certain individuals with osteoporosis.

How long should you stay on a vibration plate?

Use a vibration plate no more than two to three times a week, for up to 20 minutes at a time, being mindful not to overdo it. And, always ask your healthcare provider if this is a safe piece of equipment for your needs.

How often should you use a vibration plate? 

Roser recommends using a vibration plate one to two days per week, adding one full circuit each time. To get the most out of each session, focus on engaging your core and breathing through your belly.

Who should avoid vibration plates?

Vibration plates demand balance and stability, so those at a greater risk of falling should consult a doctor or physical therapist before use. This includes individuals with poor balance, severe osteoporosis, postural issues, limited range of motion, and strength deficits, as well as those who are pregnant, says Marko.
